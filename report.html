Financial NLP Pipeline — Analysis Report

Author

Ayush Joshi

Published

June 24, 2026

Overview

This report documents findings from an end-to-end NLP pipeline built to analyze SEC 10-K annual filings from 10 major public companies spanning 2021–2026. The system extracts named entities, classifies their context, and builds a knowledge graph to surface analytical insights from dense legal text.

Companies analyzed: AAPL, MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN, TSLA, JPM, BAC, GS, NVDA, META

Total filings processed: 34

Total chunks indexed: 18,642

Pipeline Architecture

The pipeline runs in five stages:

  1. Ingestion — 10-K filings fetched from SEC EDGAR API, cleaned of XBRL noise, chunked into 500-word segments
  2. NER — spaCy extracts entities (ORG, PERSON, GPE, MONEY, PRODUCT) with surrounding context
  3. Classification — BART zero-shot classifier categorizes each entity mention by purpose
  4. Embeddings — sentence-transformers embeds all chunks; FAISS indexes for semantic search
  5. Graph loading — Neo4j stores entities as nodes with typed relationship edges

Semantic Search Results

The FAISS semantic search correctly identifies relevant filing chunks across companies without exact keyword matching.

Supply Chain Risk

Query: “single source supplier concentration risk disruption”

Top results are dominated by Apple filings across multiple years — Apple’s 10-K has a dedicated section on single-source component dependency that surfaces consistently:

“The Company’s new products often utilize custom components available from only one source. When a component or product uses new technologies, initial capacity constraints may exist…” — AAPL 2024

AI Risk and Investment

Query: “artificial intelligence risk uncertainty investment opportunity”

META dominates — reflecting their disclosed $13.8B investment in Scale AI and extensive AI risk language. MSFT and GOOGL also surface appropriately.

Climate Risk

Query: “climate change environmental risk carbon emissions sustainability”

JPMorgan and Goldman Sachs lead — financial regulators specifically require climate risk disclosure in a structured framework (physical risk, transition risk) that produces richer climate language than tech companies.

Entity Classification Results

Geographic Exposure

The zero-shot classifier distinguishes why companies mention locations — a significant upgrade over raw mention counts.

Apple’s China exposure:

Category Mentions
Market 29
Supplier 13
Regulatory 2

Apple talks about China more as a revenue market than a manufacturing location — counterintuitive given the manufacturing narrative, but accurate given how their 10-K structures risk language.

Key geographic findings:

  • Ireland → almost exclusively regulatory across all companies (tax domicile, EU regulatory jurisdiction)
  • Taiwan → geopolitical risk and supplier (semiconductor supply chain)
  • Israel → supplier only, exclusively NVIDIA (Mellanox acquisition)
  • India → mixed market and facility (growing R&D and market presence)

Executive Classification

The PERSON classifier distinguishes between:

  • executive — normal role mentions (“Timothy D. Cook has served as CEO since 2011”)
  • key_person_risk — dependency risk language (“our business depends heavily on…”)
  • board_member — governance mentions
  • legal_party — regulatory and legal proceedings

Topic Modeling

BERTopic identified key themes across all filings including:

  • Electric vehicle competition and battery technology (Tesla-dominated)
  • Accounting standards updates (cross-company)
  • Tax position and deferred tax assets (cross-company)
  • XBRL taxonomy definitions (noise — to be filtered in future work)

Limitations and Future Work

Current limitations:

  • Zero-shot classification accuracy degrades on ambiguous financial language
  • Location entity deduplication merges common variants but misses edge cases
  • Co-mention graph is sparse — companies rarely name competitors directly in 10-Ks
  • Dataset of 10 companies is illustrative, not comprehensive

Planned enhancements:

  • Fine-tune a domain-specific NER model on financial text (FinBERT-NER)
  • Add LLM-based classification using the Anthropic API for higher accuracy on ambiguous cases
  • Expand to S&P 500 with distributed processing
  • Add temporal analysis — track how a company’s risk language changes year over year
  • Build a Streamlit dashboard for interactive exploration

Conclusion

The pipeline demonstrates that meaningful analytical signals can be extracted automatically from dense 10-K legal text. The combination of NER, zero-shot classification, semantic search, and graph traversal produces insights — such as distinguishing China’s role as market vs supplier, or identifying which companies have the richest climate risk disclosure — that would otherwise require manual analyst review of thousands of pages of filings.

The architecture mirrors the kind of data preparation pipeline that quantitative research teams use to generate alternative data signals from unstructured text sources.
